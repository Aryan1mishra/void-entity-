Introduction

An air pollution monitoring system for automobiles is designed to measure and track the levels of pollutants emitted by vehicles. It typically consists of a set of sensors placed within the vehicle's exhaust system, which detect the concentrations of various pollutants, such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter. The data collected by these sensors is then transmitted to a central monitoring system, where it can be analyzed to assess the vehicle's emissions performance and identify potential problems.
